Data Security Policies

Data security is very important to us at Maximal. The data we have been entrusted is personal, identifiable, private, and we strive to take every step we can to protect the integrity and privacy of that data. It is a large and important part of how

we build our applications and run our business. Specific policies are listed and described below, but the overall summary is that all of it is to support the ultimate end goal of the safe care of our customers’ data.

OWASP awareness

As a software company powered primarily by web apis (HTTPS), we are subject to a whole ecosystem of threats and potential vulnerabilities. The OWASP top ten list is a great resource for being up to date on the understanding of those attack vectors, so we can properly protect against them.

Regular software updates

We make sure that we are not ever running out–of–security–update software. We keep up to date on news of critical security (“zero day”) patches and apply them as quickly as possible to our server footprint

Authenticate and Authorize by default

One of the most common data leak vulnerabilities is a broken, missing, or otherwise malfunctioning auth layer. In our app, authentication is baked in by default in a middleware layer to ensure it is not accidentally skipped to developer error. Generalizing this policy, application architecture is always designed such that security will come by default.

Use proven security technologies

We may use cutting–edge or more exotic technology in some parts of our application, but NOT where security is concerned. Our security–focused sections of our architecture are always using technology that has been battle–tested, proven, and has numerous corporations in the community also using the same technology and contributing to regular updates.

Cookies
